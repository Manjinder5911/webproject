    COVID-19 Do’s & Don’t s

    Do’s

    • Cover your nose and mouth with handkerchief/tissue while sneezing and coughing.
    • Practice frequent hand washing. Wash hands with soap and water or use alcohol based hand rub.Wash hands even if they are visibly clean.
    • Consult a doctor if you feel unwell (fever, difficult breathing and cough). While visiting doctor wear a mask/cloth to cover your mouth and nose.
    • Sneeze in the inner side of your elbow and not to cough into the palms of your hands.
    • For any fever/flu-like signs/symptoms, please call District helpline number or the 24×7 helpline number 01638-260555 of the District Administration Fazilka.
    • Avoid crowded places.
    • Stay more than one arm’s length distance from persons at public place.
    • Take adequate sleep and rest.
    • Drink plenty of water/liquids and eat nutritious food.

    Don’t s

    • Do not Touch your eyes, nose or mouth.
    • Avoid shaking hands while greeting.
    • Don’t Spit at public places .
    • Don’t take medicines without consulting doctor .
    • Don’t Dispose used napkin or tissue paper in open areas.
    • Avoid Touching surfaces usually used by public (Railing, door, gate).

    FAQ's - Frequently asked questions

    Q. What are the precautions to prevent the spread of COVID-19?

    A. While it is crucial to mention hygiene standards like washing your hands frequently, especially if you have travelled by public transport. Using an alcohol sanitizer, in case you are travelling to disinfect your hands, wearing a mask (cover your nose and mouth) and avoiding touching your hand or mouth

    Q. Can COVID-19 be transmitted through food?

    A. There is currently no evidence that people can catch COVID-19 from food. The virus that causes COVID-19 can be killed at temperatures similar to that of other known viruses and bacteria found in food

    Q. What are the COVID-19 guidelines for social distancing?

    A. If you have to be around people, maintain a distance of 2 meters or 6 feet from others around you as much as possible. Not just mass gatherings, but even shopping malls, stadiums, and movie theatres can make you a target. In general, any gathering of over ten people should be avoided or conducted virtually.

    Q. How long does it take for symptoms of the coronavirus disease to appear?

    A. On average it takes 5–6 days from when someone is infected with the virus for symptoms to show, however it can take up to 14 days.
